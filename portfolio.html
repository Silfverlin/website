"The robots... they have become intelligent! And they are vengeful, thirsting for human blood!"

Summary: An experimental system of communication between autonomous entities

This project was a contract from a classified client, who requested a system that would enable autnonmous machine entities to efficiently communicate between one another to coordinate movement and various actions. Most noteworthy about this system is that the exact way in which the entities communicate was developed naturally over time, by the entities themselves. Our system only enabled the entities to gain a general capability to process communicative tokens - i.e. to realize that messages from another entity can be carried through symbols of sound patterns, gestures, wireless signals or images. The language they ended up developing seem to utilize complex combinations of communicative tokens, based on relative distance and various other circumstantial conditions. The entities themselves are described by the client as "cool looking robots, which may or may not possess a certain degree of military capability."

"Relax, it's just going to be in control of the nation's missile defense systems. Sheesh, it's just an AI, not freaking Skynet!"

Summary: An AI-controlled missile defense system

While originally much smaller in scope, early prototypes of this independently operating centrally AI-controlled defense system managed to impress our client to the point that the project was quickly scaled up to be able to operate on a national level. The AI, based on the most recent technology within self-learning artificial intellgence and developed in close cooperation with industry leaders within neural robotics, is able to completely independently coordinate and operate complex missile defense systems of sizes vastly larger than those of today's major nations. The AI can, without any kind of external user input, analyze threats and act accordingly with unprecedented speed and efficiency.
